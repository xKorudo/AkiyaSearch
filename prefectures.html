Japan's 47 prefectures

Japan is divided into 47 prefectures (都道府県), grouped into eight regions stretching from snowbound Hokkaidō in the north to subtropical Okinawa in the south. Each has its own landscape, climate, dialect, food and history. Here's a guide to all of them and what they're known for — then jump straight to the vacant akiya houses there.

These are concise overviews for orientation. Each prefecture links to its Wikipedia article for credible, in-depth information; for official travel and regional data see the Japan National Tourism Organization (JNTO).
